Slightly longer story:

I've been on the business-side/hiring manager-side for years. I started out in teaching, then moved to advertising, then to digital marketing, and eventually to be a director of web and ecommerce at a global top 10 leadership development institution. (Stay with me, I'll get there.)

After full-time roles in management in the public and private section, I've been freelancing and contracting for years now (mostly business consulting and digital marketing). But a blind person can see a clear need and opportunity in the marketplace looming on the horizon as we head to a "new normal" resulting for global business shut-downs, workforce quarantine, and deep recession. This new normal will rely heavily on distributed workforces and virtual, asynchronous service and product delivery.

But that's the businessy response. I am entrepreneural by nature, and creating is not a desire, but a need as desperate as breathing to me. I have as COO of a startup in seed rounds right now. I am a marketer (MBA with ~20 years experience), messager (art with words), graphic designer (full Adobe stack), and the one medium I haven't delved into to my satisfaction mdash; the one I've passionately wanted to for decades — is progamming and development. It is the cross-roads of creation, artful results derived from language translation, and pure utility — in which the end result isn't something just to sit back and enjoy observing, but rather is something that can be engaged with at infinitely deep levels - as deeply as the programmmer (or teams of programmers) can envision and create.

See, I truly connect with and love the HTML and CSS coding that I do (and do pretty well). But I see this as not only a necessary medium in this global environment that I must develop in order to be competitive, but quite frankly it's a necessary medium for me to master so that I can create and thrive and satisfy this impulse that drives me and draws me to programming. I simply can't ignore it. A polyglot programmer is my destiny, so to speak.
